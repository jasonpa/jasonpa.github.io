## How to choose between locations

**Consider your commute**: Choose the location closest to your home or workplace, or the one that's easiest to reach during your typical schedule.

**Work schedule**: If you need evening appointments, check which location has better availability for your preferred times.

**Multidisciplinary needs**: Both locations offer physiotherapy, massage, and other services if you need coordinated care.

**Try both**: Some patients alternate between locations based on their schedule. Both offer the same high standard of acupuncture care.

**Phone booking**: Call [insert phone numbers for each location] to speak with someone directly. This is helpful if you have questions about your specific situation, insurance, or ICBC claims.

**What to mention when booking**:
- Whether you're coming in for ICBC, extended health benefits, or private pay
- Your main concern (neck pain, headaches, stress, etc.)
- Any scheduling preferences (morning, evening, specific days)

## What to bring to your first visit

**For ICBC patients**:
- Claim number
- Date of accident
- Photo ID
- List of medications
- Any medical records or imaging reports

**For extended health benefits**:
- Insurance card or policy information
- Photo ID

**For all patients**:
- Comfortable clothing
- List of current medications
- Any questions you want to ask

## What to expect at your first visit

- Arrive 5-10 minutes early to complete any remaining paperwork
- Your first appointment will take 60-75 minutes (includes assessment and treatment)
- You'll have a detailed conversation about your health history and current concerns
- Treatment will be tailored to your specific needs
- You'll receive a personalized care plan and recommendations for follow-up
